How to use this calculator

  1. Measure the conditioned room area.
  2. Enter a planning rate that has a documented project basis.
  3. Use the output as a traceable preliminary value, then review a full load calculation before final selection.

Formula

Planning BTU/h = room area (ft²) × selected planning rate (BTU/h per ft²)

Variables and scope

Example

500 ft² × 20 BTU/h per ft² = 10,000 BTU/h as a preliminary value. The result is only as valid as the documented rate and conditions behind it.

Result interpretation

Area-based planning can help organize an early conversation, but it cannot account for windows, shading, insulation, outdoor design conditions, people, equipment, infiltration, outdoor air, or humidity. Do not represent this output as a Manual J or final design result.

Frequently asked questions

Can this replace Manual J?

No. This tool deliberately requires your own rate and does not model the inputs used in a formal load procedure. Use recognized methods and qualified review for final sizing.

What planning rate should I enter?

Use a rate with a documented project basis. This site does not supply a universal rate because climate, envelope, solar, occupancy, outdoor air, and humidity change the result.

Does the result tell me what duct size to use?

No. A preliminary load may inform an airflow estimate, but duct design also requires air-path, pressure, fitting, velocity, and equipment details.
