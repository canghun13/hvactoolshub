How to use this converter

  1. Enter the equipment or planning value in refrigeration tons.
  2. Read the equivalent BTU/h and thermal kW.
  3. Check the original rating conditions before comparing equipment.

Formula

BTU/h = refrigeration tons × 12,000
Thermal kW = BTU/h ÷ 3,412.141633

Example

1.5 refrigeration tons × 12,000 = 18,000 BTU/h. The equivalent thermal rate is 5.276 kW. This is capacity conversion only; it is not 5.276 kW of electrical input.

Result interpretation

Use this result to read capacity labels in a common unit. A ton rating does not capture climate, building load, humidity, equipment performance, or duct conditions. Verify the actual space load before selecting equipment.

Frequently asked questions

Is one HVAC ton a physical weight?

No. A refrigeration ton is a conventional thermal-capacity rate equal to 12,000 BTU/h.

Can I use tons to estimate electricity consumption?

No. Electrical consumption depends on the equipment’s efficiency and operating conditions. Use manufacturer input ratings for that question.

How many BTU/h is 2 tons?

2 × 12,000 = 24,000 BTU/h.
