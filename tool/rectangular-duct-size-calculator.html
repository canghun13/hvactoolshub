How to use

  1. Assign CFM to the specific duct run.
  2. Enter a project-selected target velocity.
  3. Enter a practical width-to-height ratio and use the output as a theoretical starting size.

Formula

Face area (in²) = 144 × CFM ÷ FPM
Height = √(area ÷ ratio); Width = ratio × height

Variables

The aspect ratio is width divided by height. It redistributes the same calculated face area; it does not make different shapes behave identically in a real duct system.

Example

At 800 CFM and 700 FPM, required face area is 164.6 in². With a 2:1 ratio, the theoretical dimensions are approximately 18.1 × 9.1 in.

Result interpretation

Select a practical available duct and recalculate its actual area and velocity. Then review equivalent diameter, material, fittings, transitions, straight length, terminals, available fan pressure, noise, and balancing. High aspect ratios can add fabrication constraints and change resistance.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use any aspect ratio?

Physical space and design criteria govern the choice. Check the selected shape with the project duct design method and complete pressure path.

Does equal face area mean equal pressure loss?

No. Geometry, roughness, fittings, and equivalent diameter influence resistance. Equal area is only the starting geometry relationship.
