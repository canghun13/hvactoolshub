How to use

Provide a verified project friction factor and air density. This calculator covers straight round duct only; it does not add fittings, transitions, dampers, or equipment losses.

Formula

ΔP = f × (L ÷ D) × (ρ × V² ÷ 2)

Variables

f is Darcy friction factor, L is length, D is diameter, ρ is density, and V is velocity. Units are converted internally to SI before reporting inches of water gauge.

Example

Enter the duct's CFM, diameter, straight length, measured or specified density, and friction factor from the selected design method.

Result interpretation

Do not treat this as total external static pressure. Add fitting losses and confirm the complete system with an appropriate duct design method.

FAQ

Why must I enter a friction factor?

Friction factor varies with duct surface, Reynolds number, and conditions; making it explicit avoids presenting a universal value as a design standard.
