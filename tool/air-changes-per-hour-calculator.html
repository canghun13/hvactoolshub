How to use this calculator

  1. Enter the inside dimensions of the space.
  2. Enter the CFM for one identified airflow stream.
  3. Use the result as the volume-flow ratio for that stream.

Formula

Room volume (ft³) = length × width × height
ACH = CFM × 60 ÷ room volume (ft³)

Variables and units

TermMeaningUnit
CFMOne stated volume-flow streamft³/min
Room volumeInside dimensions multiplied togetherft³
ACHVolume-flow ratio per hour1/h

Example

A 20 ft × 15 ft × 8 ft room has 2,400 ft³ of volume. At 240 CFM, 240 × 60 ÷ 2,400 = 6 ACH.

Result interpretation

ACH describes a volume-flow rate relative to room volume. It does not mean all air is perfectly replaced or mixed each hour. Supply, outdoor air, exhaust, return, and filtered recirculated air answer different questions, so do not substitute one stream for another without a documented basis.

Frequently asked questions

Does an ACH result set the required ventilation rate?

No. It only calculates a ratio from the values entered. Follow applicable codes, project criteria, and qualified HVAC design for requirements.

Can I use supply CFM as outdoor-air CFM?

Not unless the system documentation shows they are the same. Supply air may include recirculated air, while outdoor air is a separate quantity.

Does a higher ACH always mean better performance?

No. Air distribution, contaminants, humidity, filtration, pressure relationships, noise, energy, and system purpose all matter.
