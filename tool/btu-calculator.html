How this calculator works

HVAC capacity is commonly labeled in BTU per hour (BTU/h), while equipment literature may use watts, kilowatts, or refrigeration tons. This calculator converts those capacity units from a single normalized BTU/h value. It does not estimate a building load or an appliance's electrical input.

Formula

W = BTU/h × 0.29307107
kW = BTU/h ÷ 3,412.141633
RT = BTU/h ÷ 12,000

Frequently asked questions

Is BTU/h the same as watts?

Both express a rate of energy transfer, but they use different unit systems. One BTU/h equals about 0.29307107 W.

Does 12,000 BTU/h mean an air conditioner uses 3,517 watts of electricity?

No. It means the unit has about 3,517 W of cooling capacity. Electrical input depends on the equipment efficiency and operating conditions.

Can this tool size an air conditioner for my room?

No. Correct equipment sizing requires a load calculation that accounts for construction, climate, solar gains, ventilation, occupancy, and more.
