How this calculator works

HVAC capacity is commonly labelled in BTU per hour (BTU/h), while equipment literature may use watts, kilowatts, or refrigeration tons. The calculator first normalizes your input to BTU/h and then displays equivalent thermal rates. It does not estimate a building load or an appliance’s electrical input.

Formula

W = BTU/h × 0.29307107
kW = BTU/h ÷ 3,412.141633
RT = BTU/h ÷ 12,000

Result interpretation

Use the result to compare labels that describe the same thermal capacity. Do not read 3.517 kW thermal as electrical consumption. Electrical input varies with equipment efficiency, operating conditions, and the manufacturer’s ratings. For a space-specific capacity question, calculate the load first.

Frequently asked questions

Is BTU/h the same as watts?

Both express a rate of energy transfer, but they use different units. One BTU/h equals about 0.29307107 W.

Does 12,000 BTU/h mean an air conditioner uses 3,517 watts of electricity?

No. It means the unit has about 3,517 W of cooling capacity. Electrical input depends on the equipment and operating conditions.

Can this tool size an air conditioner for my room?

No. Correct equipment sizing requires a load calculation that accounts for construction, climate, solar gains, ventilation, occupancy, humidity, and more.
