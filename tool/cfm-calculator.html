How to use this calculator

  1. Enter the inside length, width, and ceiling height of the space.
  2. Enter an ACH value with a documented project, code, or engineering basis.
  3. Read the implied airflow in cubic feet per minute.

Formula

Room volume (ft³) = length × width × height
CFM = room volume (ft³) × ACH ÷ 60

Variables and units

TermMeaningUnit
Room volumeInside length × width × heightft³
ACHVolume changes per hour1/h
CFMVolume flow rateft³/min

Example

A 20 ft × 15 ft × 8 ft room has 2,400 ft³ of volume. At 6 ACH, 2,400 × 6 ÷ 60 = 240 CFM.

Result interpretation

The calculation converts an ACH assumption into a volume flow. It does not determine that the ACH is appropriate, nor does it distinguish total supply, outdoor air, exhaust, return, or filtered recirculated air. Those streams can have different requirements and design purposes.

Frequently asked questions

Does this set a required ventilation rate?

No. It only converts a chosen ACH to CFM. Use applicable requirements and qualified design for ventilation decisions.

Does ACH mean all air is perfectly replaced?

No. ACH is a volumetric rate. Actual mixing, distribution, short-circuiting, filtration, and contaminant removal depend on the system and space.

Can I use this result to select HVAC equipment?

No. Equipment selection needs a load calculation and manufacturer performance data. Airflow and capacity are related but are not interchangeable.
