How to use

  1. Assign CFM to the individual duct run.
  2. Enter the actual selected inside dimensions, not only a theoretical size.
  3. Use velocity as one check alongside pressure loss, fittings, noise, and distribution.

Formula

Velocity (FPM) = CFM ÷ duct face area (ft²)
Round area = π × (diameter in ft)² ÷ 4
Rectangular area = width (in) × height (in) ÷ 144

Example

600 CFM in a 14 in round duct has an area of about 1.069 ft², giving approximately 561 FPM. The same CFM in an 18 in × 8 in rectangle has 1.00 ft² face area, giving 600 FPM.

Result interpretation

Velocity is a geometry and airflow relationship, not a stand-alone approval. Higher or lower velocity can affect pressure loss, sound, distribution, space, and cost. Evaluate the complete critical path and use the applicable project design criteria.

Frequently asked questions

Does this calculate pressure loss?

No. Pressure loss also depends on duct length, fittings, surface, air properties, components, and the full air path.

What velocity should I use?

This tool does not prescribe one. Select and document a velocity basis appropriate to the system, acoustic objective, pressure budget, and applicable design method.
