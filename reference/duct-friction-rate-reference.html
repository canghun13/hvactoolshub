Duct Friction Rate Reference

Understand what straight-duct friction rate represents before using it in an HVAC design workflow.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Definition

Friction rate commonly expresses straight-duct pressure loss per unit length. It is not the total pressure loss of a duct system.

Inputs that must be stated

InputWhy it matters
Airflow and sizeThey establish velocity and Reynolds-number-related behavior
Material and surfaceThey influence friction behavior
Air densityIt changes the pressure calculation
FittingsThey add losses outside straight-duct friction

Formula

One preliminary form is ΔP = f × (L ÷ D) × (ρV² ÷ 2). Use a verified friction factor and appropriate unit conversions.
