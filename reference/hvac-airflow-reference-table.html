Formula and unit scope

CFM = room volume (ft³) × ACH ÷ 60
ACH = CFM × 60 ÷ room volume (ft³)
m³/h = CFM × 1.69901082

These formulas relate volume flow to room volume or metric volume flow. They do not provide a universal ventilation rate, supply-air quantity, fan selection, or duct design criterion.

How to use this table

  1. Measure or calculate the actual room volume, including ceiling height.
  2. Use the CFM/ACH relationship only after choosing an airflow target from the applicable design or ventilation basis.
  3. Identify the air stream: supply, return, exhaust, outdoor, or recirculated.
  4. For ducts, continue with area, velocity, friction, fittings, and available fan pressure.

Limits and source context

ASHRAE treats ventilation, infiltration, internal gains, and system effects as distinct components in HVAC design. An ACH conversion alone cannot establish air quality, comfort, humidity control, or code compliance. Confirm project requirements and equipment performance with a qualified professional.

FAQ

Does this select a fan?

No. Fan selection also requires system resistance, controls, operating point, sound, and performance data.
