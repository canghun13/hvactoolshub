What this reference converts

Btu is an energy quantity. BTU/h, watts, and kilowatts are rates. Refrigeration tons are a cooling-capacity convention. This page compares the units; it does not estimate a building’s heating or cooling load, electrical consumption, or equipment performance.

1 Btu1.055056 kJ (International Table Btu)
1 BTU/h0.29307107 W
1 kW3,412.141633 BTU/h
1 RT12,000 BTU/h

Formulas

W = BTU/h × 0.29307107
BTU/h = kW × 3,412.141633
RT = BTU/h ÷ 12,000

How to use the table

  1. Identify whether the starting value is an energy amount or a capacity rate.
  2. Use a row or formula to convert the capacity unit.
  3. For equipment selection, check manufacturer capacity at the documented design conditions.
  4. For building sizing, calculate the load first; a unit conversion does not validate the result.

Example

A 30,000 BTU/h thermal capacity is 2.5 RT and approximately 8.79 kW thermal. It does not mean the unit consumes 8.79 kW of electricity.
