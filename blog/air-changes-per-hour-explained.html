Air changes per hour explained

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

What ACH means

ACH is the hourly volume flow divided by an enclosed room’s volume. It gives a compact way to compare nominal air movement between rooms of different sizes. It does not identify the air source or guarantee that every part of the room receives the same air treatment.

Formula

ACH = CFM × 60 ÷ room volume (ft³)

For metric calculations, use consistent units: m³/h divided by room volume in m³ gives ACH directly.

Example

A 2,400 ft³ room supplied with 240 CFM has 240 × 60 ÷ 2,400 = 6 ACH. If the same 240 CFM serves a 4,800 ft³ room, it is 3 ACH. The flow has not changed; only the reference volume has changed.

Where the design still needs more information

For ventilation, the occupancy, contaminants, exhaust, outdoor-air fraction, filtration, controls, and applicable local requirements matter. For cooling and heating, supply-air temperature, sensible/latent loads, equipment performance, and room distribution matter. Use ACH as one stated quantity within that larger design context.

FAQ

Is one ACH a complete air replacement?

No. ACH is a nominal volume-flow relationship. Mixing, short-circuiting, air distribution, and outdoor-air fraction can be different.

Can ACH be used for duct sizing?

It can produce a preliminary airflow value after room volume is known. Duct sizing also requires velocity, area, pressure loss, fittings, and fan performance.
