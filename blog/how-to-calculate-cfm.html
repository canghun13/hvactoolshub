How to calculate CFM

Convert room volume and a documented air-change target into a preliminary airflow value. First identify the air stream and the basis for the target.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Formula

CFM = room volume (ft³) × ACH ÷ 60

Room volume is length × width × ceiling height. ACH must come from a defined project, occupancy, process, or applicable requirement; it is not a universal HVAC default.

Step-by-step calculation

  1. Measure the room length, width, and finished ceiling height in feet.
  2. Multiply them to get room volume in cubic feet.
  3. Choose the stated ACH basis and identify whether the result represents supply, exhaust, outdoor, or recirculated flow.
  4. Multiply volume by ACH and divide by 60.
  5. Check the result against the system’s load, duct, fan, sound, and pressure requirements.

Worked example

A room is 20 ft long, 15 ft wide, and 8 ft high. Volume is 20 × 15 × 8 = 2,400 ft³. If a documented design basis calls for 6 ACH, CFM is 2,400 × 6 ÷ 60 = 240 CFM. The calculation converts the stated basis to flow; it does not prove that 6 ACH is appropriate for every room.

Common calculation errors

ErrorCorrection
Using floor area instead of volumeInclude ceiling height; ACH is based on volume.
Mixing ft³ with m³Keep the entire calculation in one system before converting the final airflow.
Calling every airflow outdoor airIdentify supply, return, exhaust, outdoor, and recirculated streams separately.
Selecting a duct from CFM aloneCheck area, velocity, friction, fittings, and available fan pressure.

FAQ

Can this replace a ventilation design?

No. It is a volume-flow conversion. Ventilation design also needs the applicable requirements, air-stream definition, equipment, and distribution review.

How do I convert CFM to m³/h?

Multiply CFM by 1.69901082. For 240 CFM, the result is about 408 m³/h.
