How Duct Velocity Affects HVAC Performance

Velocity is airflow divided by duct area, and it affects space, noise, and pressure-loss tradeoffs.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Velocity begins with area

At a fixed CFM, smaller duct area produces higher FPM. A larger area produces lower FPM.

Higher velocity tradeoffs

Higher velocity can make ducts smaller but may increase pressure loss and acoustic concerns. It must be evaluated alongside the full system.

Project criteria control

No single FPM target is right for every run. Use the project design method, equipment limits, and applicable requirements.

FAQ

Can this guide select a design velocity?

No. It explains the relationship; a qualified design must select criteria.
