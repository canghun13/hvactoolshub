HVAC Psychrometrics Explained

Psychrometrics connects temperature, moisture, energy, and pressure for one stated air condition.

Created 2026-07-20 · Updated 2026-07-20

The direct answer

Psychrometrics is the study of moist-air properties. In HVAC it helps describe whether air must be cooled, heated, humidified, dehumidified, or simply moved.

Properties work together

PropertyMeaningHVAC use
Dry bulbAir temperatureSensible condition
Relative humidityVapor pressure relative to saturationState comparison, not moisture mass alone
Dew pointSaturation temperature for the moisture stateCondensation risk context
Humidity ratioWater-vapor mass per dry-air massMoisture balance
EnthalpyMoist-air energy per dry-air massTotal-air process comparison

Example

At 24°C, 50% RH, and 101.325 kPa, the Psychrometric Calculator derives a dew point, humidity ratio, enthalpy, and approximate wet bulb for one condition. Changing pressure changes humidity ratio and enthalpy; relative humidity alone is not enough.

Wet bulb versus dry bulb

Dry bulb is the measured air temperature. Wet bulb describes an evaporative cooling equilibrium and depends on pressure. The calculator labels its wet-bulb result as a constant-enthalpy approximation, not a substitute for an instrument-specific measurement method.

Common mistakes

  • Comparing RH values at different dry-bulb temperatures as if they represent equal moisture content.
  • Ignoring pressure or elevation when using humidity ratio.
  • Using dew point or wet bulb alone to select equipment without a complete load calculation.
