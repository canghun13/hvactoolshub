Round vs Rectangular Ducts

Shape changes the available space, fabrication options, and friction behavior of a duct run.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Round duct

Round duct is commonly efficient for conveying air because it has no corners, but its diameter may not fit a shallow ceiling or chase.

Rectangular duct

Rectangular duct can use constrained height or width efficiently. Its dimensions should be checked with an equivalent diameter or a selected friction method rather than area alone.

Compare the actual system

Space, fittings, leakage control, insulation, acoustic criteria, fabrication, and cleaning access all affect the best choice.

FAQ

Does equal area mean equal pressure loss?

No. Different shapes can have different friction behavior at the same face area.
