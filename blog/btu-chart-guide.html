BTU chart guide

How to use an HVAC BTU chart

A capacity chart lets you compare equivalent units. It does not tell you what size system a particular room needs.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Read the time basis first

An HVAC chart should normally say BTU/h, not just Btu. BTU/h is a heat-transfer rate used for capacity. Check whether adjacent columns show thermal kW, refrigeration tons, or electrical data before comparing them.

Use a chart in the right order

  1. Calculate or obtain the required heating/cooling load with the appropriate method.
  2. Use the chart to express that thermal rate in the units used by a specification.
  3. Check manufacturer performance data at the design conditions.
  4. Review airflow, duct distribution, humidity, and local requirements before selecting equipment.

What a BTU chart cannot show

A chart does not account for solar exposure, envelope performance, outdoor air, humidity, occupants, ceiling height, ducts, or equipment performance. Room-size rules added to generic charts are screening shortcuts, not a substitute for a documented load calculation.

FAQ

Can a BTU chart select equipment?

No. It converts or compares capacity; final selection requires a suitable load calculation and manufacturer performance data.

Why does a chart list kW?

kW provides a metric expression of thermal capacity. Confirm that it is labeled as capacity, not electrical input.
