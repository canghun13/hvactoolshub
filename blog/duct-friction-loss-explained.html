Duct Friction Loss Explained

Pressure drops as air moves through ductwork; straight duct, fittings, and system components all contribute.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

What friction loss represents

Friction loss is the pressure reduction associated with air moving through a duct surface and flow path. Fan selection must account for the total system resistance.

What changes the result

Velocity, length, diameter, duct material, air properties, fittings, and transitions matter. Fittings are not interchangeable with an equal length of straight duct.

Use transparent assumptions

When using a preliminary formula, record air density and the selected friction factor or design method so another reviewer can reproduce the result.

FAQ

Is friction loss the same as total static pressure?

No. Total system pressure also includes fittings, terminals, equipment, and other components.
