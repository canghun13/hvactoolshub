How many CFM do I need per room?

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

There is no single room-CFM number

A room may need supply air for heating/cooling, outdoor air for ventilation, return air for circulation, and exhaust for a specific use. Those requirements can overlap, but they are not the same calculation. Start by naming the air stream and the purpose.

Use room volume for a volumetric estimate

CFM = room volume (ft³) × documented ACH ÷ 60

For a 12 ft × 15 ft × 9 ft room, volume is 1,620 ft³. At a stated 4 ACH planning basis, the conversion is 1,620 × 4 ÷ 60 = 108 CFM. This is not a default recommendation; it is only the flow corresponding to the stated basis.

What must be checked before using the result

QuestionWhy it changes room CFM
Is the flow supply, return, outdoor, or exhaust?Each air stream has a different system role and may have a separate requirement.
What is the room load?Solar, envelope, people, equipment, humidity, and supply-air conditions influence cooling/heating airflow.
How is the room connected to the system?Return path, transfer air, door position, and balancing affect delivered airflow.
What do the ducts and fan allow?Area, velocity, friction, fittings, sound, and available pressure constrain the flow.

A practical room-airflow workflow

  1. Measure the room volume and identify its use.
  2. Separate heating/cooling supply from outdoor-air, exhaust, and return questions.
  3. Use a documented design basis to convert volume to a preliminary flow if appropriate.
  4. Check the room load and the whole-system air balance.
  5. Check duct velocity and pressure loss before changing grilles or duct sizes.

FAQ

Can I use cooling airflow as outdoor air?

No. Cooling supply, outdoor ventilation air, return air, and exhaust air are different airflow categories and must be evaluated separately.

Does a larger room always need proportionally more CFM?

Not always. Volume matters for an ACH conversion, but load, occupancy, solar exposure, equipment, and airflow purpose can change the room requirement.
