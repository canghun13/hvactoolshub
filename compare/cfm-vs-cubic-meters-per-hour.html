CFM vs cubic meters per hour

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Quick answer

CFM and m³/h both measure volume flow. CFM uses cubic feet per minute; m³/h uses cubic meters per hour. Converting units changes the number, not the airflow stream, fan performance, or design requirement.

Use the unit conversion safely

Keep the same air-stream label before and after conversion. For example, 240 CFM supply air becomes about 408 m³/h supply air; it does not become outdoor ventilation air. When a calculation uses velocity, area, pressure, or room volume, convert every associated input consistently.

FAQ

Are CFM and m³/h equivalent airflow rates?

Yes. They express the same volume-flow rate in different units after conversion.

Can I use a converted flow to choose a duct size?

Only as one input. Duct design also depends on shape, area, velocity, pressure loss, fittings, and the available fan pressure.
