CFM vs air changes per hour

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Quick answer

CFM states how much air moves each minute. ACH states how many room-volume equivalents move in an hour. The same 240 CFM is 6 ACH in a 2,400 ft³ room, but only 3 ACH in a 4,800 ft³ room.

Formula and example

ACH = CFM × 60 ÷ room volume (ft³)
CFM = room volume (ft³) × ACH ÷ 60

For a 20 ft × 15 ft × 8 ft room, volume is 2,400 ft³. A 240 CFM flow corresponds to 240 × 60 ÷ 2,400 = 6 ACH. That formula says nothing about whether the 240 CFM is supply, return, outdoor, exhaust, or recirculated air.

Choose the measure that matches the question

  • Use CFM when a fan, duct, grille, or system component has a stated airflow.
  • Use ACH when comparing air movement with the size of one enclosed room.
  • Use the applicable standard or local requirement for ventilation targets; do not apply a generic ACH value to every occupancy.
  • For cooling and heating, use a load and supply-air analysis in addition to a room-volume conversion.

FAQ

Which should I use: CFM or ACH?

Use CFM for a specified air stream or equipment flow. Use ACH when the purpose is to compare a flow with a defined room volume.

Does 6 ACH mean all room air is replaced six times?

No. ACH is a nominal volume-flow relationship. Actual air distribution, mixing, short-circuiting, and outdoor-air fraction can be different.
