BTU/h vs HVAC tons

They express thermal capacity at different scales; neither one is a load calculation or an equipment-selection approval.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-20

Quick answer

BTU/h is a thermal capacity rate. Refrigeration tons are a larger HVAC capacity convention: 1 RT = 12,000 BTU/h. For example, 36,000 BTU/h equals 3 RT. Use the unit that matches the specification, while checking actual capacity at applicable conditions.

Choose the useful unit

Use BTU/h when you need the exact thermal rate. Use tons when discussing nominal HVAC capacity classes. If the question is what capacity a building needs, start with a project-specific load method; unit conversion comes afterward.

FAQ

Is one HVAC ton a weight?

No. It is a refrigeration-capacity convention, not equipment weight or airflow.

Does a 2-ton system always deliver 24,000 BTU/h?

Not necessarily. Delivered capacity depends on equipment, configuration, and operating conditions. Use manufacturer performance data for selection.
