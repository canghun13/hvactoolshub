BTU vs HVAC Tons

Both express capacity. The difference is the scale and the way HVAC equipment is commonly labeled.

Created 2026-07-16 · Updated 2026-07-16

Quick answer

BTU/h gives a detailed capacity rate. Refrigeration tons are a convenient HVAC shorthand: 1 RT equals 12,000 BTU/h.

When to use BTU/h

Use BTU/h when a specification, calculation, or equipment label gives a specific heat-transfer rate.

When to use HVAC tons

Use refrigeration tons when comparing nominal cooling capacities across common equipment classes.

FAQ

Is 1 HVAC ton equal to one ton of equipment weight?

No. It is a cooling-capacity unit.
